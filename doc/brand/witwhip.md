## Genesis

The name emerged through a multi-stage exploration:

1. **Working title** — the repository started as `cognitive-bias-tester`, a functional but
   unbrandable descriptor.

2. **Philosophical detour** — a parallel naming session with Gemini explored deeper concepts:
   heart + mind, truth + love, Taoist philosophy (ZhiXin 知心, SentioRatio). Many candidates
   were philosophically rich but either taken (ZhiXin.io, Ametas.com) or too long to say aloud
   (SentioRatio — 5 syllables).  
   Reference: `doc/external/name_conversation_gemini.md`

3. **WitWhip proposed** — the name was introduced independently and chosen over all alternatives
   for its combination of memorability, semantic fit, and confirmed availability.

4. **Availability verified** — zero web presence found; no trademark conflicts; witwhip.com
   registered immediately.

---

## Name Anatomy

```
Wit  +  Whip
 │        │
 │        └─ to whip into shape — train, sharpen, discipline
 └─────────── intelligence, sharpness of mind, critical faculty
```

**Core reading:** *Whip your wit into shape.* Train the parts of your mind that cognitive
biases exploit.

**Secondary reading:** A whip is a quick, precise motion — snapping a lazy thought pattern
into focus.

---

## Taglines

Ordered from most to least recommended:

1. **"Train your critical thinking."** — clear, universal, works as a subtitle everywhere
2. **"Spot the trap. Sharpen your mind."** — two-beat rhythm, evokes the quiz format
3. **"Your biases are showing."** — provocative, memorable, works for social media
4. **"Think before you think you're thinking."** — playful, for onboarding/marketing copy
5. **"Sharp minds aren't born. They're trained."** — aspirational, good for landing pages

## Visual Identity Notes

*(To be developed — these are early anchors, not final decisions)*

- **Initials:** WW — strong double letterform for icons and favicons
- **Color direction:** High-contrast; sharp edges; avoid soft pastels (wrong tone)
- **Typography:** Geometric sans-serif — confident, not corporate
- **Logo concept:** The two W's can suggest a whip crack or a brainwave — worth exploring
- **Avoid:** Anything that looks like a fitness app or a car accessory (both use "whip")

---

## What This Brand Is NOT

- Not a mindfulness / meditation app — no soft language, no "journey"
- Not an academic tool — no jargon without explanation
- Not a gamified trivia app — the point is insight, not points
